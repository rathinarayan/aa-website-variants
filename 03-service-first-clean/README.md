# Variant 03 · service-first-clean

The minimalist read. StoryBrand structure most visible. Grade 6 language throughout.

## Thesis
Strip everything that is not essential. Hero is one sentence and one button. Immediately after the hero, three service cards ask the visitor "which one sounds like you?". The whole page is a conversation that walks a buyer from recognition to a booked call in four scrolls.

## What it borrows
- StoryBrand walk: problem → three plans → proof → CTA. Most explicit of the three variants.
- Precision's diagnose → prescribe → prove, same as variant 02, but simpler language
- Numbered prompts for every step so the eye always knows where it is
- One accent colour per block (orange over white, purple over soft, white over ink)

## What it leaves
- Editorial italics (variant 01 owns those)
- Builder-voice quote walls (variant 02 owns those)
- Any AI-native or role-replacement headline; every headline is a plain-English invitation
- Case studies as a story; here they are a four-stat block instead

## Copy moves unique to this variant
- One-sentence hero, no sub-claim, no eyebrow flourish
- Service cards phrased as <em>"Which one sounds like you?"</em>
- Middle service card (AI pipeline) rendered in AA purple as the only accented card to signal it is the service line most buyers ask about
- AI Solutions rendered as three coloured blocks (diagnose / prescribe / prove) instead of prose
- Finale is a second CTA that reuses the hero button verbatim

## Who it is for
Visitors who cannot stay on a site more than 90 seconds. Owners who want to know the cost and the deliverable before they book a call. Non-native English readers.

## Open items visible in this page

2. Why Cubed account manager name missing
